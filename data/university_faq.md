# UDSM Student Support FAQ

> Demonstration FAQ for the IS 365 assignment. Not official University of Dar es Salaam policy.

## Course Registration

UDSM students register for courses each semester through the **UDSM ARIS** student portal at https://aris.udsm.ac.tz (demo reference).

Steps:
1. Log in with your student registration number and password.
2. Open **Course Registration** during the official registration window (usually the first two weeks of semester).
3. Select courses for your programme and confirm registration.
4. Print or save your registration slip for your records.

Add/drop changes are allowed only during the add/drop period announced by your college. Contact your academic advisor if a required course is full.

## Examination Rules

UDSM examinations are governed by university examination regulations and the **Committee on Discipline (CODe)**.

Key rules:
- Arrive at least 30 minutes before the exam with your student ID and examination card.
- Mobile phones, smart watches, and unauthorized materials are not allowed in the exam room.
- Students must sign the attendance sheet and follow invigilator instructions.
- Bring only permitted materials (e.g. calculator if allowed, pens, student ID) as stated on the exam paper.
- Any form of cheating, impersonation, or disruption may lead to disciplinary action.
- If you are ill on exam day, report to your college office immediately with medical documentation.

## Library Services

The **UDSM Library** at Mlimani campus provides study spaces, borrowing services, and online resources.

Services:
- Opening hours: Monday–Friday 08:00–20:00, Saturday 09:00–17:00 (demo hours).
- Search books using the library OPAC catalogue.
- Undergraduate students may borrow up to 4 books for 14 days.
- Access e-resources on campus or via the library portal with student credentials.
- Quiet study zones are available on upper floors; group discussion rooms may be booked at the help desk.

## ICT Support

The **UDSM ICT Unit** supports student email, Wi-Fi, and portal access.

Common support topics:
- Reset student email password through the ICT helpdesk or online self-service portal.
- Campus Wi-Fi: connect to the UDSM network using your student credentials; use eduroam where available.
- ARIS / student portal login issues: verify registration number and contact ICT if locked out.
- For exam materials or past papers, check with your college office or library — not all are online.
- ICT helpdesk location: Mlimani campus (demo). Email: ict@udsm.ac.tz (demo).

Bring your student ID when visiting the ICT office for in-person support.

## Hostel Application

UDSM hostel accommodation is allocated to eligible students based on availability and university policy.

Application process (demo):
1. Check hostel application announcements on the UDSM website and student portal.
2. Complete the online hostel application form when the window opens.
3. Pay the required hostel deposit through GePG if selected.
4. Collect hostel allocation letter from the Dean of Students office.
5. Report to your assigned hall (e.g., Mlimani or college hostel) on the published check-in date.

First-year and distant students may receive priority depending on annual policy.

Hostel fees (demo figures — confirm current amounts with the Dean of Students office): approximately **TZS 150,000 per semester** for a standard shared room and **TZS 250,000 per semester** for a single room. Fees are paid as a deposit through GePG once a hostel allocation is confirmed.

## Fee Payment

UDSM fees are paid through approved payment channels including **GePG** (Government e-Payment Gateway), **NMB**, and **CRDB**.

Steps:
1. Obtain your control number from the student billing section or ARIS.
2. Pay at a bank branch, mobile banking, or online using the control number.
3. Keep the payment receipt until your student account is updated.
4. Confirm payment status in ARIS after 24–48 hours.

Late payment may attract penalties or block registration. Contact the finance/bursar office for payment disputes.

## Academic Calendar

UDSM operates on a semester system (demo calendar):

- **Semester I:** October – February (registration week in October)
- **Semester II:** March – July (registration week in March)
- **Supplementary examinations:** August–September (where applicable)

Key dates are published on the UDSM website and notice boards. Students should check college announcements for programme-specific schedules.

## Student Conduct

UDSM students must follow university regulations and maintain academic integrity.

Expectations:
- Respect staff, fellow students, and university property.
- Dress and behave appropriately on campus.
- No harassment, discrimination, or violence.
- Disciplinary cases are handled through college and university disciplinary committees.
- Serious misconduct may result in suspension or expulsion.

Report misconduct to your college administration or the Dean of Students office.

Appeals (demo process): a student may appeal a disciplinary committee decision in writing to the College Disciplinary Committee within 7 days of being notified. If unresolved at college level, the appeal may be escalated to the University Disciplinary Committee (CODe).
